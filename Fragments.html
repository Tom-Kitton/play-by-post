TEXTUAL FRAGMENTS
Being writings pertaining to this world through which we wander
History and Geography

Read this first; for it contains a brief gloss of the recent history and present political organisation of the Queen's Chain. This is a recommended text for those encountering this world for the first time.

A map of The Queen's Chain and Nearby Lands.

A collection of travelogues, journeying guides and observations from the road can be found here. These describe many of the diverse and particular regions of the Queen's Chain, as experienced by famous travellers of recent years.

Newspapers

A rendering of several leader articles from the Universal Register of Shipping, into plain text, can be found here.

An account of an awful crime, originally published in the Mail and Globe, can be found here.

A collection of cookery instructions, arts reviews and literary notices from the Universal Register of Shipping can be found here.

The article reproduced herein describes a scandalous outrage on the part of corrupt magnates in the fen country of Emmslee. The article was originally published in the Common Cry, of Deeswick, a wallpaper of a radically reforming character, and it is indicative of the great fellow-feeling and political solidity of the Emms folk, as well as the black-hearted foolery of many mercantile people who take their dripping bread from the sorrows of working people.

Academic Texts

A scholarly collation of fragments from a larger mystical work, known as the Last Book of Kirkwood, can be found here. This text was found in abandoned monastic library near Roode, the so-called "keephouse library" referred to in the horrible Tale of Claude and the Book of Numbers.

A description of a magical process by which a type of evil creation known as an "Egg Devil" might perhaps be created can be found here.. This short description, a copy, is the only publicly-known survival of the great corpus of magical writing created by the Tannist monks of Old Morreres, who were vigorously suppressed in the era of reconciliation, and remain greatly feared for their supposed evil powers.

Governmental Publications

A proclamation of interdiction, issued by the Deaconry against a particular political society of the labouring classes can be found here. Many such proclamations were issued by the Deaconry prior to the establishment of the Writ of Due Dealings in ER. 219, which allowed common protection to societies of political agitation.

A Patience of Freestanding, issued to a traveller in The Queen's Chain, can be found here. Such patiences are legal instruments by which foreign strangers who sojourn within the lands of the chain may receive variegated and specific protections of some laws applicable to the chain's own citizens. Patiences are issued on a graduated scale of rights and protections, their exact content depending on the trust in which the person in question is held by the Queen's deputised agents in the Departmental Service for Strangers' Affairs

Some pages from an official gazette of the Queen's Chain, issued during the later years of the Hilliers Regency, can be found here. The gazette collects useful and educative information and articles demonstrating the collective common-sense of the educated classes.
